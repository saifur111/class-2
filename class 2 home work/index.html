AL leader beaten to death over 'irregularities' in relief distribution -

A local Awami League leader was beaten to death by some youths following an altercation reportedly over irregularities in relief distribution at Garduara in Hathazari upazila of Chattogram on Wednesday, reports UNB. The deceased was identified as Bakhtiar Shikder, 48, general secretary of Garduara union unit Awami League and son of Manir Ahmed of the upazila.Lokman, a UP member of ward no. 7 under Gorduara union and also the elder brother of the victim said when Rana, son of Abdul Halim, alleged on Tuesday that the people of his area are not getting relief Bakhtiar opposed it. They locked into an altercation over the issue which was later settled by local people. On Wednesday afternoon, Rana along with three associates swooped on Bakhtier, leaving him critically injured. He died on the way to hospital in the night.

Fareast International University VC dies of COVID-19 -

A former teacher of Dhaka University (DU) and also vice-chancellor of a private university, Fareast International University, died of COVID-19 on Thursday morning. Professor Nazmul Karim Chowdhury was undergoing treatment at Dhaka Medical College Hospital. The university’s Board of Trustee’s chairman Sheikh Kabir Hossain confirmed this to Prothom Alo. DMCH director brigadier general AKM Nasir Uddin told Prothom Alo that professor Nazmul was earlier undergoing treatment at a private hospital.He was admitted to Dhaka Medical College Hospital three days ago after being diagnosed with coronavirus infections. From the beginning he was in the ICU, he added. Nazmul Karim Chowdhury was a former professor of Dhaka University. DU proctor AKM Golam Rabbani said Nazmul Karim Chowdhury was a former professor in the Department of Tourism and Hospitality Management at the university.

'Crack Platoon' offers free rides to physicians, caregivers -

To offer free rides to the physicians and other health caregivers to their workplaces amid the nationwide coronavirus lockdown, Crack Platoon Transport Service kicked off its journey at the beginning of April of this year.
Thank you Crack Platoon, your help means a lot... It was really impossible to attend duty from a place like BeriBadh to Suhrawardy Medical as a female doctor at night...
Physician Sadia Ahmed
With 40 cars and 10 minibuses, the platform has provided 19,000 free rides to the COVID-19 front liners in last one month, stated a press communiqué on Thursday.The press statement said, Starting with 40 big and small cars and 10 minibuses providing service in three shifts per day in 16 routes, Crack Platoon Transport Service has completed over 4,500 trips in three shifts per day in 22 routes in more than a hundred hospitals inside Dhaka City. Almost 1,800 physicians and caregivers have registered 19,204 times on Crack Platoon Transport Service’s website to avail the service in this period.

Faridpur jute growers in troubled waters amid pandemic -


The jute growers in Faridpur, a top jute-producing district of the country, are in deep trouble due to a severe shortage of farm workers amid the ongoing shutdown enforced to curb the novel coronavirus disease outbreak. Farmers feared less production as they are not being able to hire labourers to weed their land, and their woes have worsened with the growing pest attacks. Jute, a fibre that can be spun into sackcloth, is a brand product of Faridpur and its branding slogan is ‘Sonali Ashe Bharpur, Bhalabasi Faridpur’ (which can roughly be translated as – we love Faridpur, an area full of golden fibre).The farmers of the district have been cultivating jute from generations due to its fertile soil and favourable weather. According to the DAE, this year some 82,996 hectares of land have been brought under jute cultivation and the farmers are expecting a good yield.Nurunnabi, a farmer of Kaichail union in Nagarkanda upazila, said, This year, the jute yield looks to be very good. Now it’s time to weed our land but we don’t get any workers due to the coronavirus outbreak. Last year, I hired a worker at Tk 300 a day but now the workers who are available demanding Tk 600 a day - just double!.Obaidul, another farmer, said, It’s the high time for weeding jute field as pest has attacked the leaves of jute. Timely weeding is needed for their proper growth.

Upazila health complex in Barishal put under lockdown -

The Local administration on Wednesday night put Gournadi upazila health complex and 25 houses in Batajor Bandar in Barishal under lockdown after a nurse tested positive for coronavirus, reports UNB. Upazila administration issued a notice signed by Upazila nirbahi officer Israt Jahan in this regard. The notice said the upazila health complex and 25 houses in Batajor Bandar area were under lockdown as a senior nurse of the hospital was diagnosed with coronavirus.The area is overpopulated, said UNO Israt Jahan adding that legal action will be taken if anyone violates the lockdown directives. BDF, a forum of physicians, said 375 nurses of government and private hospitals were infected with coronavirus as of Wednesday. Meanwhile, 612 physicians were infected while treating patients, said BDF.Bangladesh has recorded 11,719 coronavirus cases and 187 deaths as of Wednesday. The country reported its highest daily new coronavirus cases on Wednesday with the detection of 790 patients. Even though the government extended general holidays until 16 May, it has eased the restrictions on movement by allowing shopping malls, shops, and markets to operate.The government also allowed businessmen to keep shops and shopping malls open for six hours from 10:00am to 4:00pm every day from 10 May, after maintaining health guidelines during the general holidays.
